ABOUT ME

Hi! My name is Elaine and I am a third year year computer science major at California Polytechnic State University. My main technical focus full-stack web development and my other areas of interest include artificial intelligence, and virtual reality.

After discovering my passion for web development, I decided to start participating in hackathons to collaborate with other students to use technology to tackle social problems. So far this year, I have developed several web apps intended to improve the lives of people in our community, such as increasing safety of students living on campus, helping families with children who have autism, and providing an extra resource for students with hearing impairments.

I have worked part time as an IT programmer student assistant and have created a web application to simplify the process of applying for student government at Cal Poly. I am currently interning year-round at Amazon, working with React, Java, and DynamoDB to create new features for an internal app.

Asides from web development, I am also highly involved in the women in technology community at Cal Poly through clubs such as Society of Women Engineers (SWE), and Women Involved in Software and Hardware (WISH). I am also on the directors team of UX Fest, which is Cal Poly's first UX competition founded by women of color that aims to create an inclusive space for students to learn more about UX.

My side passions include photography, video editing, desigining, and making piano covers.
